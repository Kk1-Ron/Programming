Recent Trends in Computing

What is Cloud Computing? Explain its types.

Cloud computing is an on-demand delivery of IT resources and applications over the Internet with pay-as-you-go pricing. Instead of owning and maintaining computing infrastructure yourself, you can access services like servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence over the internet from a cloud provider. It offers scalability, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness by converting capital expenditure into operational expenditure.

Types of Cloud Computing Services:

  • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS): This provides fundamental computing infrastructure resources like virtual machines, networks, storage, and operating systems over the internet. Users have high control over their infrastructure.
    Example: Amazon EC2, Microsoft Azure Virtual Machines.
  • Platform as a Service (PaaS): This offers a complete development and deployment environment in the cloud, with resources that enable developers to build, run, and manage applications without the complexity of building and maintaining the infrastructure typically associated with developing and launching an app.
    Example: Google App Engine, Heroku.
  • Software as a Service (SaaS): This delivers fully functional applications over the Internet, managed by a third-party vendor. Users simply access the software via a web browser or mobile app.
    Example: Gmail, Salesforce, Microsoft 365.

What is Mobile Computing? Explain its advantages and disadvantages.

Mobile Computing refers to a technology that allows users to access information and applications from remote locations using wireless devices and networks. It involves the use of portable computing devices (like smartphones, tablets, laptops) and wireless communication technologies (like Wi-Fi, 4G, 5G) to perform tasks on the go, facilitating real-time data access and communication regardless of the user's physical location.

Advantages of Mobile Computing:

  • Portability: Users can access information and perform tasks from anywhere, without being tied to a fixed location.
  • Connectivity: Enables continuous connection to the internet and other networks, facilitating real-time communication and data access.
  • Productivity Enhancement: Allows professionals to work remotely, respond to emails, access files, and collaborate, improving overall productivity.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Reduces the need for physical infrastructure and can optimize resource utilization.
  • Location-Based Services: Facilitates services like GPS navigation, location-aware advertising, and emergency services.

Disadvantages of Mobile Computing:

  • Security Risks: Mobile devices are prone to data breaches, malware, and unauthorized access due to their portability and constant connectivity.
  • Limited Resources: Mobile devices often have smaller screens, limited processing power, and battery life compared to desktop computers.
  • Connectivity Issues: Reliance on wireless networks means performance can be affected by signal strength, network congestion, and coverage gaps.
  • Interoperability Challenges: Different mobile operating systems and device specifications can lead to compatibility issues with applications and services.
  • Health Concerns: Prolonged use can lead to eye strain, repetitive strain injuries, and other health-related issues.

What is E-commerce? Explain its types.

E-commerce (Electronic Commerce) refers to the buying and selling of goods or services using the internet, and the transfer of money and data to execute these transactions. It involves online retail, electronic funds transfers, supply chain management, internet marketing, online transaction processing, electronic data interchange (EDI), inventory management systems, and automated data collection systems.

Types of E-commerce:

  • Business-to-Consumer (B2C): This is the most common type, where businesses sell products or services directly to individual consumers online.
    Example: Amazon, Daraz, Foodmandu.
  • Business-to-Business (B2B): This involves businesses selling products or services to other businesses online. Transactions are typically larger in volume and complexity.
    Example: Wholesalers selling to retailers, software companies selling enterprise solutions to other companies.
  • Consumer-to-Consumer (C2C): This type of e-commerce facilitates transactions between individual consumers, often through third-party platforms that host these exchanges.
    Example: eBay, HamroBazaar (classifieds where individuals sell to other individuals).
  • Consumer-to-Business (C2B): In this model, individual consumers offer goods or services to businesses.
    Example: Freelancers offering their skills (writing, design, programming) to companies on platforms like Upwork or Fiverr; individuals selling stock photos to businesses.

What is E-governance? What are the objectives and challenges of it.

E-governance (Electronic Governance) refers to the application of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) for delivering government services, exchange of information, communication transactions, integration of various stand-alone systems and services between government-to-citizens (G2C), government-to-businesses (G2B), government-to-government (G2G), and government-to-employees (G2E). The primary aim is to improve the efficiency, transparency, and accountability of governance.

Objectives of E-governance:

  • Improved Service Delivery: To provide better, more convenient, and faster access to government services for citizens and businesses.
  • Enhanced Transparency and Accountability: To make government operations and decisions more open and transparent, reducing corruption and promoting accountability.
  • Citizen Empowerment: To provide citizens with more information and opportunities to participate in the governance process.
  • Efficiency and Cost Reduction: To streamline government processes, reduce bureaucratic hurdles, and lower operational costs.
  • Reduced Digital Divide: To extend government services to remote and underserved areas, bridging the gap between those with and without access to technology.

Challenges of E-governance:

  • Digital Divide: Unequal access to ICT infrastructure and digital literacy among different segments of the population.
  • Security and Privacy Concerns: Protecting sensitive citizen data and ensuring secure online transactions is a major challenge.
  • Lack of Infrastructure: Insufficient or unreliable internet connectivity, power supply, and hardware in many regions.
  • Resistance to Change: Government employees and citizens may resist adopting new digital systems due to unfamiliarity or fear of job loss.
  • Legal and Regulatory Framework: Absence of clear laws and policies to govern electronic transactions, digital signatures, and data protection.
  • Interoperability Issues: Difficulty in integrating various government departments' systems that operate on different platforms and databases.

What is Big Data? Explain its characteristics.

Big Data refers to extremely large, complex, and diverse datasets that cannot be easily processed or analyzed using traditional data processing applications. These datasets are so voluminous that they require advanced and unique data management technologies and tools for efficient storage, analysis, and visualization. The primary goal of Big Data is to extract valuable insights, patterns, and trends to enable better decision-making.

Characteristics of Big Data (The 5 Vs):

  • Volume: This refers to the immense amount of data generated every second. Traditional databases cannot handle such massive scale of data, requiring new technologies for storage and processing.
    Example: Billions of sensor readings, petabytes of social media posts.
  • Velocity: This refers to the speed at which data is generated, collected, and processed. Data streams in at unprecedented speeds and must be processed quickly to be valuable.
    Example: Real-time stock market data, online gaming interactions, streaming video.
  • Variety: This signifies the diverse types of data formats. Big Data includes structured (database tables), semi-structured (XML, JSON), and unstructured data (text, images, audio, video).
    Example: Text from emails, images from surveillance cameras, audio from voice assistants.
  • Veracity: This refers to the trustworthiness, quality, and accuracy of the data. Big Data often comes from various sources, and its reliability can be inconsistent, requiring robust data cleansing and validation processes.
    Example: Inaccurate sensor readings, fraudulent social media posts.
  • Value: This is the most crucial characteristic, referring to the ability to transform raw big data into meaningful and valuable insights that can drive business decisions and innovations. Without value, the other Vs are meaningless.
    Example: Identifying customer purchasing patterns, predicting disease outbreaks.
